Introduction

Social Issues

 
        Throughout its evolution, social medias main aim has been to find new ways to keep people in contact with one another constantly 
        updating and adapting the way communication occurs with aims for interactions to be more entertaining and inclusive. 
        However, due to the easy exposure to content, many social problems have occurred during the evolution. In March 2023, 
        a census was taken by ‘The Office for National Statistics’ focusing on the different ways bullying occurs online 
        among children aged 10-15 years of age. The study showed that 92.6% of children went online daily with 58.1% spending 3 or more hours online during a school day which was 
        an increase of 10.5% from results taken in March 2020. An estimated 19.1% of children experienced online bullying with 18.1% of those kids choosing not to tell anyone about 
        what they were facing. 35% of children accepted a friend request from someone they did not know making them susceptible to receiving abuse from those who share no consideration 
        about the way they may be feeling. (Office for National Statistics). All these figures have increased or remained constant from the data taken in March 2020 from which we can interpret 
        that the issue is constantly rising, and not enough measures are being taken to prevent these rises in values. It has also been seen that social media affects self- esteem and was seen 
        the teens are more anxious especially due to the pressure of texting. The lack of in-person interaction has made teenagers unable to interpret body language and facial expressions leading 
        to misunderstanding and hurt feelings. With some experts thinking that connecting with peers online is less emotional than connecting in person. FOMO is another contributing factor which can intensify 
        the feelings of loneliness and self-esteem giving feelings that ithers are having fun or living a better life than you are. Therefore, promoting that feeling of addiction even at risky times. 
        (Dalomba, 2022) (Hillard,2024)
